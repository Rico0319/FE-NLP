Slide 1: 10-K MD&A Data Extraction & Cleaning

Data Source: SEC EDGAR API. Downloaded S&P 500 10-K filings for fiscal years 2019-2025. Parsed HTML to extract Item 7 (MD&A) sections. Handled filing variants (10-K, 10-K/A, 10-K405) and missing MD&A cases.

Section Boundary Detection:
- Start: Item 7 "Management's Discussion and Analysis"
- End: Item 7A "Quantitative and Qualitative Disclosures About Market Risk" or Item 8 "Financial Statements"
- Removed pseudo-headings from table of contents to avoid mixing adjacent sections

Text Cleaning Pipeline:
- Stripped HTML/XBRL tags
- Removed headers, footers, table fragments, navigation strings, encoding noise
- Standardized whitespace and line breaks
- Lowercase conversion without aggressive stemming
- Preserved negation words, modal verbs (may, could, expect, plan), numbers, and percentages
- Reason: Subsequent generic vs. substantive classification depends on sentence semantics, implementation status, management tone, and firm-specific linkage, not just keyword frequency

Sentence Segmentation:
- Split MD&A into sentence-level observations using spaCy
- Adapted to 10-K text features: long sentences, semicolons, parentheses, bullet residuals, broken lines
- Not simple period-based splitting; combined rule-based tokenizer with manual inspection logic
- Rationale: Same paragraph often contains both broad strategic language and concrete operational detail; sentence-level analysis prevents classification noise from mixed units

Deduplication & Quality Control:
- Removed exact duplicate sentences
- Removed near-duplicate sentences
- Deleted heading sentences, fragment sentences, and overly short sentences
- Deleted cross-reference fragments that do not form complete semantic units
- Reason: Avoid boilerplate language or format repetition inflating disclosure intensity

AI Candidate Pre-filtering (for Group B):
- Applied seed dictionary to flag sentences containing AI-related terms
- Goal: High recall, not high precision
- Purpose: Provide candidate pool for Group B's classification work
- Not responsible for generic/substantive judgment

Output: mdna_sentence_master.jsonl
- ~1.96 million sentence rows
- Fields per sentence:
  - Firm identifiers: cik, gvkey, ticker, company_name, filing_date, fiscal_year, accession_number
  - MD&A metadata: section_name, mdna_total_word_count, mdna_total_sentence_count, mdna_total_char_count
  - Sentence fields: sentence_id, sentence_order, sentence_raw, sentence_clean, sentence_word_count
  - Quality flags: is_exact_duplicate, is_near_duplicate, keep_sentence_flag
  - AI candidate support: ai_candidate_flag, ai_keyword_matched_terms

- Liquidity: cash_ratio, current_ratio, quick_ratio

- Cash flow: fcf_to_assets, cash_holdings, wc_to_assets

Future Performance Variables (t+1 lead variables):
- future_ROA: groupby(gvkey).shift(-1) on ROA
- future_sales_growth: groupby(gvkey).shift(-1) on sales_growth
- future_earnings_growth: (ni_t+1 - ni_t) / abs(ni_t)

- future_rd_to_assets: groupby(gvkey).shift(-1) on rd_to_assets

CRSP Market Variables:

- volatility_12m: 12-month return standard deviation
- Coverage: 80.3% (2,681 / 3,338) due to CRSP coverage gaps

Winsorized Versions:

Slide 3: Critical Data Cleaning Challenges & Fixes

Challenge 1: Ticker Mismatch Between NLP and Compustat
- NLP uses hyphens: BRK-B, BF-B
- Compustat uses dots: BRK.B, BF.B
- Fix: Pre-merge ticker normalization map
  {'BRK-B': 'BRK.B', 'BF-B': 'BF.B', 'GOOG': 'GOOGL', 'NWS': 'NWSA', 'FOX': 'FOXA'}

Challenge 2: Fiscal Year Misalignment for Non-December FYE Companies
- Problem: NLP fiscal_year (filing year) != Compustat fyear for Jan-May FYE companies
- Example: BF-B FY2019 (May 2018-Apr 2019) has datadate=2019-04-30 but Compustat fyear=2018
- Naive merge on fiscal_year == fyear matches to wrong year's data (2-year lookahead bias)
- Affected: 42 tickers (~8% of S&P 500), 269 rows
- Fix: For Jan-May FYE companies, adjust merge key: controls_fyear = NLP_fiscal_year - 1
  Implementation: Parse datadate month, apply fyear_adj = fiscal_year - 1 when fye_month in [1,2,3,4,5]

Challenge 3: Dual-Class Share Duplicates
- Problem: Same 10-K filed under two tickers (GOOG/GOOGL, FOX/FOXA, NWS/NWSA)
- Creates duplicate accession_numbers, double-counting in regressions
- Detection: groupby(accession_number).size() > 1
- Fix: Keep primary class (has gvkey/cik in Compustat), drop secondary
  GOOG -> keep GOOGL, FOX -> keep FOXA, NWS -> keep NWSA
  21 duplicate accession numbers resolved, 21 rows dropped

Challenge 4: gvkey Zero-Padding in WRDS PostgreSQL
- Problem: WRDS stores gvkey as VARCHAR(6) with leading zeros ("001690")
- Python pandas reads as integer (1690) or float (1690.0)
- Passing "1690" to psycopg2 IN clause matches nothing for 5-digit gvkeys
- Fix: Always apply str(int(float(x))).zfill(6) before WRDS queries
- Impact: Without fix, supplemental variable coverage dropped from 98% to ~50%

Challenge 5: CRSP-IBES Linkage for Dual-Class Shares
- Direct ticker merge fails for dual-class shares (different ticker conventions)
- Correct path: gvkey -> permno (CCM) -> IBES ticker (ibcrsphist)
- Implementation:
  Step 1: crsp.ccmxpf_lnkhist for gvkey-permno mapping
  Step 2: wrdsapps_link_crsp_ibes.ibcrsphist for permno-IBES_ticker mapping
  Step 3: ibes.statsum_epsus for consensus forecasts

Challenge 6: Missing gvkey/cik for Hyphenated Tickers
- BF-B and BRK-B had all gvkey/cik = NaN after initial merge
- Root cause: Controls CSV uses BF.B and BRK.B, NLP uses BF-B and BRK-B
- Fix: After ticker mapping, fill gvkey/cik from controls lookup
  BF-B: gvkey=2435, cik=14693
  BRK-B: gvkey=2176, cik=1067983
